## Three Proof Bullets

- **19-phase MPTE: the only continuous pentest engine in a CTEM platform.** The Micro Pentest Engine (MPTE) moves through Reconnaissance → Enumeration → Identification → Exploitation Preparation → Controlled Exploitation → Evidence Collection → Lateral Assessment → Cleanup → Reporting. Every phase is bounded with automatic rollback on anomaly. Compliance output meets SOC 2, PCI-DSS 11.3, and NIST SP 800-115. (Source: docs/CTEM_PLUS_IDENTITY.md §MPTE, suite-attack/)
- **FAIL Engine: chaos engineering for AppSec — industry first.** The Fault & Attack Injection Layer (FAIL) injects synthetic attack patterns, simulates scanner failures, grades organizational response time and accuracy, and generates labeled training data for ML improvement. No ASPM, SAST, or CTEM competitor ships an equivalent capability. (Source: docs/CTEM_PLUS_IDENTITY.md §FAIL Engine, competitive_validation_2026-04-26.md §0)
- **Attack-path graph shows lateral movement, not just individual CVEs.** attack_path_engine.py and attack_graph_gnn.py construct a graph neural network-backed attack graph — red teamers can drill into blast radius, chained exploit paths, and pivot potential across the asset inventory, not just single-finding CVSS scores. (Source: suite-core/core/attack_path_engine.py, attack_graph_gnn.py)
