## What "consolidated" actually means right now

The 48 `*Hub.tsx` files ARE imported and routed. But they are **not the primary nav surface**. The sidebar `navSections` in `WorkspaceLayout.tsx` exposes **163 individual leaf links** across 26 collapsible groups under 6 section tabs. A user landing on the app sees:

- **6 section tabs** at the top of the sidebar (Discover / Protect / Respond / Comply / Intelligence / Platform)
- Click any section → **3–5 collapsible group headers** appear (e.g., "Vulnerability Management", "Identity & Access")
- Expand any group → **3–10 individual page links** drop down

This means a user navigating the sidebar can reach **163 distinct pages** without ever typing a URL. The hub pages exist as routes but many are not the primary sidebar entry points — the sidebar still links directly to the leaf pages the hubs were supposed to absorb.

## Why the Founder Sees "So Many Screens"

1. **Hubs exist as routes but the sidebar bypasses them** — the `navSections` data in `WorkspaceLayout.tsx` (lines 161–528) lists the old leaf-page routes directly. Hubs are reachable by URL but the sidebar never puts them as the primary nav entry.

2. **163 sidebar leaf items across 26 groups** — even with section-tab filtering showing only one section at a time, each section expands 5–10 groups × 5–10 items = 25–50 visible links per section.

3. **Duplicate routes for the same domain** — API security has 3 routes, threat feeds has 2, compliance has 2 (`/comply` and `/compliance`), risk acceptance appears twice, security scorecard appears twice.

4. **The 349 redirects mask actual bloat** — nearly 60% of all `<Route>` entries are `<Navigate>` redirects, meaning the old routes still exist and are individually accessible via URL even after consolidation attempts.
